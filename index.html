Services

This is what matimpole Arts offers.

Singing and poetry

The use of songs and chants is the way to enhance the play, our power play is to combine music with acting(musical) the music blends well with the act on stage to execute a nice piece, every song has a purpose on stage and that is what Matimpole Arts produces when singing. We prose to narrate, describe, argue, or define words to make the audience produce an intense emotion that is joy, sorrow, anger, catharsis, love through poetry.

Choreographed dancing

On all our perfomances we use choreographed dances to produce appealing stage plays, every dance has a meaning and it contributes to the part being portraited on stage, most of our dances are sanctioned to specific artistic movement, mostly we use contemporary dance but infused with the African rythm.

Acting

Our fully skilled members of the group have been well taught to use their emotions, gestures and voice variations when acting to captivate the viewer's attention, by combining singing, dancing and acting, a powerfull perfomance is guaranteed.

About Us

What is Matimpole Arts?

  • Since 2014

    Our Humble Beginnings

    Matimpole Arts Group is a theatre group based in Gaborone, Botswana, We make all kinds of performances covering different styles of approach and genres. whether it be comedy, tragedy, satire presented using ambush theatre / street theatre, arena or proscenium stage approach; we will musically portray a marvellous performance. Through artistic perseverance we developed a motive that make us to be a unique versatile arts entity. We have worked with different companies, organizations, institutions and individuals through out the past years and our aim is to expand and work with other stakeholders nationally and internationally. Currently we are a group of 25, 13 male performers and 12 female performers. Our mandate entails coming up with creative ideas and innovations that can be executed using performing arts.!

  • WHAT DO WE DO NOW?

    Way forward

    Matimpole Arts since being formed in 2014 chose a committee that leads the team even now, we started attending more and more performances in the industry, most of the time we came out victorious in competitions, our talent was recognized earlier this year as we were called by the creators of the earth hour convention to come and perform the flash mob as a way of enticing people to switch off all the lights jus for an hour to save electricity, the opportunity encouraged us to keep moving forward, we ended up making our own show which was a success!

  • WHAT ARE THE PROPS USED?

    How do you infuse them in the perfomance?

    We combine the djembes, guitars, shakers, marimba and the piano to produce appealing stage performances, we make musical plays most of the time which is our style, we also produce dance pieces!

  • TO DATE

    what now?

    Matimpole is now a fully capable theatre group that can handle any performance at any level, we are booked to perform at any time any day, we have a high level artistic director and members who can do almost anything artistically!!

Love in the making

My Matimpole, my culture.

Synopsis; She was a noble village girl who came to the city looking for a better life, she was also under pressure from her uncle who wants her to get married and have babies, Nonofatso met a young man in mag city who then wanted them married in the city, but she and her family where willing to do things the way their ancestors did, Nono encountered problems from her past as mysteries unfolds, her family fought first with her fiance's family for bringing money instead of cattle, the matter is then settled and a solution is found, Nono and her Man get married and the story ends. Percussions, dances and songs where used to enhance this piece!

  • Date: February 2015
  • Venue: Little Theatre National Museum

Se dulee

Sephiri se dule (mysteries unfolds).

A story about a girl and a boy who where dating in school, his father (Mr. Marco) was a well respected man in the village, her father (Rragwe Star) is a drunkard who is always at the shebeen, the boy's father gives alcohol to the girl who then impregnates her, secrets unfold when the man is brought to the kgotla to answer for his crimes, the magistrate sent two police officers to go and arrest Mr. Marco finding him at the meeting with the chief they then take him away.

  • Date: July 2016
  • Venue: Chedu Choga hall(Francistown)

Moloi

Re bolaa moloi (We kill the witch)

Synopsis; A story is based on a metaphor called the witch, there is a young man (Victor) and his woman(Stella) who are in love, Stella wants to make love to Victor but he shows no interest as a way to avoid unwanted pregnancy and some sexual transmitted infections, Stella had affairs with other man as secrets unfold she then breaks up with Victor breaking him to the point of him wanting to kill himself, his mother visits him in a dream telling him that he should not try to kill himself, it is not his fault he is actually doing the right thing protecting himself, the metaphor (the witch) was revealed, Victor protected himself from STI's, unwanted pregnancy, death and destruction. Percussions, dances and songs where used to enhance this piece!

  • Date: June 2015
  • Venue: Mahalapye Community hall(Mahalapye)

Ke le drama

I'm an Artist (a theatre artist).

Synopsis; In this piece a young theatre artist (Sbudah) is taken for granted with his group, they are called to perfom but get paid with T-shirts, water bottles and no money but peanuts, he is trying to register his group(Matimpole) but it requires money, the story takes an error turn when Sbudah tries to steal a tablet from passers by in a mall, they catch him and beat him up, but then he talks in tears about the problem he is having with the society, no one takes theatre seriously, they have the talent the passion and the time to work on the arts but they always get nothing in return, the mob felt compassion for him and left him there, while he was talking someone took pictures of him and a video then they created a page on face book, a page for theatre artists who are taken for granted so that Batswana put out atleast any penny for the guy to register his group, then a certain multimedia company (Mellow Deeds) saw this page and decided to get in touch with him and his group offering them a chance to perform in their next project which is a series of a soapy, they told them about the money that the page generated, Sbudah and his group become happy as the boy's tears where heard at last, they registered their group and worked on their project.

  • Date: September 2015
  • Venue: Orapa Multipurpose hall(Orapa)

Wai Wai

My Africa, my land.

Synopsis; This is a story about a man who grew up in the mountains of Matimpole in Africa, He is the son of the earth who likes and respects his culture. This man was born on a Sunday, anyone born on a Sunday is a king, after he was raised still a boy he was initiated in a certain ritual that grooms boys to become man ( Bogwera and Bojale), after finishing this ritual the next step is to find himself a woman to marry, he finds the girl of his dreams and marries her under his culture then he has a reception not any kind of reception but the matimpole way of receipting, but he is a man now and he comes to realize that the world is not what it seems, there is too much hate, jealousy, greed and pride, this things suppresses the culture and he is worried that the kids of tomorrow will never know their culture, he takes his guitar and sings the songs of Africa asking and pleading for the society to change their ways, he poetically speaks about the culture of us as Matimpole and Africa at large. The use of poetry, percussions, choreographed dancing and singing where used to enhance this piece.

  • Date: September 2015
  • Venue: West wood hall Mantlwaneng (Gaborone)

Flash mob

The invasion.

The earth hour initiative committee asked Matimpole arts to make a flash mob on almost all the shopping centres of Gaborone(Botswana) in order to make people aware of the hour whereby electricity will be cut off just to save power. We danced and sang captivating our viewer's attention, the message was heard throughout all our performances during the day, we made 4 performances in just 1 day

  • Date: March 2015
  • Venue: River walk, Main mall, BBS mall and Railpark mall Gaborone (Botswana)
